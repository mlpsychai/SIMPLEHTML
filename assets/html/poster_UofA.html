Autism Moon: A 52-Week Journey

Sean Mapoles, M.A.
Arizona Leadership Education in Neurodevelopmental and Related Disabilities

Introduction

Problem

Autism spectrum disorder diagnosis is followed by a period of family adjustment where identity, relationships, and imagined futures are reconciled. Despite evidence that cognitive reappraisal, social support, and shared meaning-making facilitate reconciliation, no resources are provided to families to scaffold this process over time, contributing to family member isolation, identity disruption, and dysregulated adjustment.

Purpose

Autism Moon: A 52-Week Journey is a community-sourced bibliotherapy intervention for families following a child's autism diagnosis. The book provides 52 weeks of content contributed by members of the autism community, designed to strengthen family cohesion, resilience, and communication.

The Study

Study Overview

Intervention 52-week bibliotherapy guide: art, poetry, psychoeducation, emotion
Contributors Paid contribution ($300) recruited through the nationwide LEND network
Accessibility Braille strip included on print edition; accessible audio on digital edition
Design Single-group longitudinal, repeated measures across 5 time points
Sample N = 80 parents/caregivers; child ASD diagnosis within 24 months
Instruments BFRS (16 items) + FRS6 (6 items); 22 items, ~5 min administration
Time Points Pre (Week 1), Post (Week 52), Year 3, Year 5, Year 10

Sustainability

Digital Access in Perpetuity

Free download hosted on HF Spaces with custom domain. No ongoing grant dependence for distribution.

Donation Return to Funder

All proceeds will be directed toward the APF, where they will be held in an Autism Research Fund. The fund will be available for future translation research.

Print Revenue Funds Hosting

The $1 margin and donations create a self-sustaining loop that outlives the initial grant period.

Research Infrastructure at $0

QR-based data collection via UofA REDCap (HIPAA-compliant, institutionally supported). Statistics via Python. No fees.

10-Year Longitudinal Data

Extended follow-up funded by earmarked incentives ($6,000 total across Years 3, 5, and 10). Produces a dataset unmatched in the autism family intervention literature.
