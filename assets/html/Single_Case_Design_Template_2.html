Visual Analysis Criteria

Six dimensions for evaluating single-case data (Kratochwill et al., 2010).

Level

Mean performance within a phase. Compare phase means to determine magnitude of effect.

Trend

Slope of data path within a phase. Accelerating, decelerating, or zero-celerating.

Variability

Spread of data around the mean or trend line. Less variability = greater confidence in observed effects.

Immediacy of Effect

Change at the phase boundary. Abrupt level change at the phase line strengthens causal inference.

Overlap

Proportion of data points shared across phases. Less overlap = stronger evidence of treatment effect.

Consistency

Replication of effects across phases or tiers. Similar patterns across replications = experimental control.

Design Selection Guide

Design When to Use What It Demonstrates
A-B Pre-post only; no withdrawal possible Association between IV and DV
A-B-A-B Reversible behavior; strong control needed Functional relationship
Multiple Baseline Irreversible behavior or ethical concerns with withdrawal Generalization across contexts
Changing Criterion Shaping; gradual, incremental behavior change Dose-response control
